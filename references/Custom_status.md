## ✅ Good Example (Follows the Guidelines)

#### Task: Application Workflow

*   **Before Start**

*   Not Submitted

*   **In Progress**

*   Entering Data

*   Waiting for Submission

*   Waiting for Approval

*   **Completed**

*   Data Entered

*   Submitted

*   Approved

*   **Issue Occurred**

*   Returned for Revision

*   Requires Correction

*   Submission Failed

✅ Key Points

*   Actions and states are clearly defined (it is clear who is doing what)
*   During progress, “-ing” and “waiting for” are used appropriately
*   Completion is clearly distinguished using terms like “completed” and “approved”
*   Issues clearly indicate the need for rework or errors

***

## ❌ Bad Example (Does Not Follow the Guidelines)

#### Task: Application Workflow

*   **Before Start**

*   Not Started (too abstract; unclear what is required)

*   Initial State (meaning is vague)

*   **In Progress**

*   Processing (unclear what is being processed)

*   In Progress (ambiguous state)

*   Checking (unclear who is checking)

*   **Completed**

*   OK (meaning is vague)

*   Finished (weak definition of the state)

*   Done (unclear what is done)

*   **Issue Occurred**

*   NG (cause and action are unclear)

*   Error (not specific)

*   Returned (unclear what needs to be done)

❌ Issues

*   **Actions are not specific (unclear who should do what)**
*   **State granularity is inconsistent (unclear distinction between progress, waiting, and completion)**
*   **Naming conventions (e.g., “-ing”, “waiting”, “completed”) are not followed**
*   **Next actions are not clear to the user**
